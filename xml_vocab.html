XML vocabulary design project

The vocal music interpretation vocabulary is a vocabulary for describing a "grouping of information" about the interpretation, analysis, and performance of a single piece of classical vocal music, written for either voice and piano or voice and orchestra. It will describe the title, the author(s), instructions or suggestions regarding performance of the piece of music, the translation and interpretation of the text, voice types for which the piece would be appropriate, and where this "grouping of information" can be located. This type of "grouping of information" may be found in a published document or in the form of notes (commentary appended to a piece of music) taken in the form of prose, music notation, or pronunciation symbols. It is possible that some of these terms will not exist or will not be included in a given instance of the "grouping of information."

Defining the Terms

  • Name of piece: The name of the piece of music (also known as an aria, lied, mélodie, chanson, canción, art song, or song).
  • Name of work: The name of the larger work of music from which the piece of music originates.
  • Name of composer: The name of the person who wrote the piece of music.
  • Name of writer: The name of the person who wrote the text, also known as the librettist or lyricist.
  • Text source: The name of the larger work of prose from which the text originates.
  • Appropriate voice type: The particular type of singing voice (also known as the Fach) for which the piece would be appropriate in performance.
  • Original language of text: The language in which the text (as intended to be sung in this particular piece of music) was written.
  • Text in original language: The text of the piece, in its original language.
  • Text in English translation: If the text was not originally written in English, the full (or partial) translation of the text into English.
  • Pronunciation symbols: The full or partial conversion of the text to the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) or other phonetic notation.
  • Name of interpreter: The name of the person who interpreted the meaning behind the elements of the piece of music.
  • Interpreter commentary on the composer: Relevant background information about the life of the composer.
  • Interpreter commentary on the music: Information to assist the performer(s) in the precise realization of the music, including tempo, dynamics, phrasing, rhythmic subtleties, coloring, nuance, and appropriate performance practice.
  • Interpreter commentary on the poet: Relevant background information about the life of the poet.
  • Interpreter commentary on the text: Information to assist the performer(s) in the precise realization of the text, including literary interpretation, pronunciation irregularities, accentuation of important words, rhythmic subtleties, appropriate performance practice, and recommendations for keeping the text intelligible while maintaining a beautiful tone.
  • Interpreter commentary on the historical context: Information about the social and historical events that may have played a part in the creation of the poetry or music of the piece.
  • Location: A description of where the "grouping of information" can be found.
